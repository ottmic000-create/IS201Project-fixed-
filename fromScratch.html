My case as to why the St. Louis Cardinals are fun to watch

(despite being terrible)

Growing up, my dad passed on a love for baseball. I played on t-ball teams from the time I could walk. Some of my favorite memories were sitting on a couch watching the St. Louis Cardinals play, and occasionaly when visiting my Dad's family, actually go see them in person. Since those days, the Cardinals have gone from great to okay, but my love and appreciation have only gone up. Here are five reasons why I am still excited every day to turn on a cardinal baseball game.

  1. Jordan Walker and JJ Wetherholt
    • Jordan Walker is the hottest hitter in baseball this year. Although he's had a rough past two years, people have forgotten he is only 23 years old, and was a top prospect before being rushed to the league. Now, it seems like he's found his stride. With an MLB leading eight homeruns, plus a .328 batting average, .386 on base percentage, and a .736 slugging, he's off to the hottest start for a Cardinal since Albert Pujols.
    • JJ Wetherholt is the arrival of the future, and a piece the rebuild will be built around. Immediately coming from Triple A ball, he was thrust into an important role hitting leadoff and playing second, and he has not dissapointed. His stellar defense has singled him out, with 5 outs above average only 16 games into the season. His bat is warming up, with flashes of legendary potential.
  2. Comeback Victories
    • The Cardinals have already made 8 comeback victories of 2 or more runs. This offense knows how to make a comeback, and with a young roster fired up to make an impression, the bats will always keep Cardinals fans hopeful for a W. This season so far, they are perfect in extra innings going 4-4, with 3 being walk-offs at home.
  3. Baseball in general is beautiful
    • People love sports for their athleticism, and the sheer power. That's why football and basketball are so widly appreciated. What I love about baseball is the "game of inches", the precision required for elite play. A Mason Miller dotted fastball really can be called "paint", and a 450 Vladimir Guerrero foot homer really can be called art. If a pitch is off by a few centimeters, or a swing an inch too low, a whole game can change, and I find that beautiful.
  4. Because watching baseball and calculating stats makes me happy
    • Some people like scrolling, or watching movies. I find peace watching baseball. Simple as that. I love how you know the odds, and cheer against or for them. I love how you can have stats that seem meaningful like batting avergage be usurped by xOBP or WAR. So fun.
  5. Great home atmosphere at Busch Stadium
    • St. Louis is a baseball town. From the history, to the dramatic moments, to the arch in the background, Busch Stadium and its fans will never disappoint. In fact, it is commonly believed that St. Louis is the classiest organization - and they aren't wrong.
